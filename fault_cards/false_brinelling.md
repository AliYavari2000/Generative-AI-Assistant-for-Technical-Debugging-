## Symptoms
- Damage may only become obvious after the bearing returns to service
- Can later resemble localized surface damage with noise and vibration

## Likely causes
- Vibration of a stationary bearing during transport or storage
- Small-amplitude oscillatory motion
- Poor lubrication

## Expected vibration signatures
- No strong online signature while stationary unless external vibration is measured directly
- When in service later, may show roughness or localized defect-like impacts

## Confirm tests
- Check storage/transport history
- Inspect raceways/rollers for false-brinell marks
- Differentiate from true brinelling by history and damage appearance

## Fixes
- Secure shaft and housing during transport
- Pack inner and outer rings separately when appropriate
- Reduce vibration by preloading
- Use proper lubricant

## Notes
Important real-world card, but vibration-only diagnosis is weak unless contextual metadata is available.
