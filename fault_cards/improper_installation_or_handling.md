# Improper installation or handling damage

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **ID** | BRG-020 |
| **Category** | root_cause |
| **Observability from Vibration** | medium |
| **Diagnostic Confidence** | high |
| **Affected Components** | all |
| **Aliases** | mounting damage | installation damage | handling-induced bearing damage |

## Symptoms
- Immediate elevated vibration after installation
- Visible dents or brinelling marks on raceways
- Reduced bearing life well below L10 prediction

## Likely Causes
- Applying force through rolling elements during press-fit
- Dropping or impacting bearing during handling
- Contamination introduced during assembly
- Incorrect mounting tool or method

## Vibration / Diagnostic Signatures
- Broadband vibration elevation from installation
- Possible defect frequencies from brinell indentations on raceway

## Confirmatory Tests
- Vibration baseline comparison immediately after installation
- Visual inspection for handling marks or debris
- Review of installation procedure and tooling used

## Recommended Fixes
- Retrain maintenance personnel on proper bearing handling and mounting procedures
- Use correct hydraulic or thermal mounting tools (induction heaters, hydraulic nuts)
- Implement clean assembly area and contamination control protocol

## References
1. SKF Mounting and Dismounting handbook
2. FAG "Rolling Bearing Damage" publication WL 82 102
3. ISO 15243
