## Symptoms
- Heat generation and rising vibration
- Premature wear or seizure-like progression
- Discoloration or melting may be seen after teardown

## Likely causes
- Incorrect assembly preload
- Thermal growth not accounted for
- Incorrect fit/stack-up

## Expected vibration signatures
- Broadband roughness and elevated overall vibration
- May coexist with misalignment-like or inner-race-related stress patterns

## Confirm tests
- Check assembly procedure, endplay/preload specs, and thermal growth assumptions
- Inspect for raceway discoloration at ball pitch intervals
- Correlate with temperature increase

## Fixes
- Reset preload to specification
- Re-evaluate fit and thermal expansion assumptions
- Replace bearing if damage is present

## Notes
Keep separate from seizure because excessive preload is a common actionable cause rather than only an end-state.
